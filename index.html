About Us

A Warm Welcome to Cubtopia Early Learning

At Cubtopia Early Learning, we believe that every child deserves the very best start in life, and that begins with exceptional early education. We offer premium care and learning experiences for children aged six months to nine years, guided by their unique interests, developmental needs, and potential.

Our centres are purposefully designed to be inspiring, nurturing environments where children feel confident, capable, and ready to explore. With a strong focus on education, wellbeing, and meaningful relationships, we partner closely with families to support each child's individual journey, from their first day to their transition to school.

Our Inspiration

At our preschool, we are inspired by the simple yet powerful dream of creating the kind of school we would want for our own children — a place where every child feels safe, valued, and inspired to grow. We believe that the values and morals nurtured at this early stage become the foundation for life, shaping children into kind, confident, and responsible individuals. Our aim is not only to prepare them for the next steps in their academic journey, but also to guide them in becoming compassionate human beings who will carry these lessons throughout their lives.

Our Vision

"To nurture curious, confident, and compassionate lifelong learners by providing a joyful and safe environment where every child's individuality is celebrated."

Our Mission

Our mission is to:

  • Create a warm, stimulating, and inclusive learning space that fosters creativity, exploration, and a love for learning.
  • Support holistic development by focusing on cognitive, emotional, social, and physical growth through play-based and skill-based learning.
  • Partner with families and the community to build strong foundations for future academic success and responsible citizenship.
  • Encourage values of respect, kindness, and collaboration to help children grow into caring individuals."

Our Educators

Cubtopia educators are passionate professionals who bring experience, warmth, and dedication to their roles every day. More than just teachers, they are mentors and trusted guides on each child's early learning journey.

Montessori-Inspired

Self-directed, hands-on learning with practical life skills that build independence and self-discipline.

  • Independent Learning: Children choose activities and pace.
  • Hands-On Materials: Concrete to abstract (e.g., counting beads).
  • Practical Life: Washing fruits, shoes on/off, organising.
  • Outcome: Independence, self-discipline, love for learning.
Reggio Emilia-Inspired

Inquiry-led, project-based learning with the environment as the third teacher and rich documentation.

  • Projects: Long-term inquiries build critical thinking.
  • Documentation: Visible learning journeys.
  • Expression: Art, music, movement to communicate.
  • Outcome: Creativity, collaboration, reflective thinking.
Play-Based Learning

Imaginative, active and sensory play that develops creativity, social skills, and motor development.

  • Imaginative: Role-play for creativity and language.
  • Active: Running, play gym, dancing for gross motor.
  • Sensory: Water, sand, textures for cognition.
  • Outcome: Problem-solving, EQ, healthy motor growth.
Skill-Based Learning

Core competencies across literacy, numeracy, 21st‑century, social‑emotional and physical skills.

  • Cognitive: Early literacy and numeracy.
  • 21st Century: Creativity, critical thinking, collaboration.
  • Social-Emotional: Self-awareness, empathy, regulation.
  • Physical: Yoga, dance, fine motor.
  • Outcome: EI, flexibility, collaborative independence.
Value Education

Character, inclusivity, environmental stewardship and peaceful conflict resolution.

  • Character: Honesty, perseverance, teamwork.
  • Inclusivity: Respect diversity, global views.
  • Environment: Sustainability and stewardship.
  • Conflict: Peaceful resolution and perspective-taking.
  • Outcome: Responsible, compassionate citizens.
Physical Activity

Physical development is crucial to a child’s growth, and we ensure that Physical Activity is embedded into the daily routine. Children are provided with opportunities to develop both fine and gross motor skills, which are essential for overall health and well-being.

  • Gross Motor Skills: Activities like running, jumping, climbing, and sports help children build strength, coordination, and balance.
  • Fine Motor Skills: Activities such as drawing, building with blocks, or using tools help children develop dexterity and hand-eye coordination.
  • Yoga & Movement:These activities help children build body awareness, improve flexibility, and enhance self-regulation.
  • Dance & Rhythm: Movement-based activities, including dance and rhythm games, promote physical fitness while encouraging creativity and self- expression.
  • Outcome:Children develop physical strength, coordination, and self-regulation, which enhances their ability to focus, learn, and interact positively with others.
STEM-Based Learning

Our curriculum incorporates a strong emphasis on STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics), aimed at developing curiosity and critical thinking skills. STEM activities encourage children to explore the world around them, engages in problem-solving, and apply their understanding of basic scientific principles.

  • Science Exploration: Children explore concepts like cause and effect, nature, weather, and the properties of materials through hands-on experiments and outdoor exploration.
  • Technology Integration:Age-appropriate tools like tablets, coding toys, and interactive learning games are used to introduce children to technology in a way that enhances their creativity and learning.
  • Engineering and Building: Activities like building structures with blocks, creating simple machines, or designing their own inventions help children develop spatial awareness, problem-solving, and engineering skills.
  • Mathematical Thinking: Concepts like counting, measuring, sorting, andpattern recognition are integrated into everyday activities, helping children develop a strong mathematical foundation.
  • Outcome: Children develop problem-solving skills, curiosity about the natural world, and the ability to think critically and analytically—skills that are vital for success in the 21st century.
