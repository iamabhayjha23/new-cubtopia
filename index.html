About Us

A Warm Welcome to Cubtopia Early Learning

Welcome to Cubtopia Early Learning

At Cubtopia Early Learning, we believe that every child deserves the very best start in life, and that begins with exceptional early education. We offer premium care and learning experiences for children aged six months to nine years, guided by their unique interests, developmental needs, and potential.

Our centres are purposefully designed to be inspiring, nurturing environments where children feel confident, capable, and ready to explore. With a strong focus on education, wellbeing, and meaningful relationships, we partner closely with families to support each child's individual journey, from their first day to their transition to school.

Our Inspiration

At our preschool, we are inspired by the simple yet powerful dream of creating the kind of school we would want for our own children — a place where every child feels safe, valued, and inspired to grow. We believe that the values and morals nurtured at this early stage become the foundation for life, shaping children into kind, confident, and responsible individuals. Our aim is not only to prepare them for the next steps in their academic journey, but also to guide them in becoming compassionate human beings who will carry these lessons throughout their lives.

Vision Statement

"To nurture curious, confident, and compassionate lifelong learners by providing a joyful and safe environment where every child's individuality is celebrated."

Mission Statement

Our mission is to:

  • Create a warm, stimulating, and inclusive learning space that fosters creativity, exploration, and a love for learning.
  • Support holistic development by focusing on cognitive, emotional, social, and physical growth through play-based and skill-based learning.
  • Partner with families and the community to build strong foundations for future academic success and responsible citizenship.
  • Encourage values of respect, kindness, and collaboration to help children grow into caring individuals."

Our Educators

Cubtopia educators are passionate professionals who bring experience, warmth, and dedication to their roles every day. More than just teachers, they are mentors and trusted guides on each child's early learning journey.

Curriculum

Beyond books — skills that last a lifetime

Montessori-Inspired

Self-directed, hands-on learning with practical life skills that build independence and self-discipline.

  • Independent Learning: Children choose activities and pace.
  • Hands-On Materials: Concrete to abstract (e.g., counting beads).
  • Practical Life: Washing fruits, shoes on/off, organising.
  • Outcome: Independence, self-discipline, love for learning.

Reggio Emilia-Inspired

Inquiry-led, project-based learning with the environment as the third teacher and rich documentation.

  • Projects: Long-term inquiries build critical thinking.
  • Documentation: Visible learning journeys.
  • Expression: Art, music, movement to communicate.
  • Outcome: Creativity, collaboration, reflective thinking.

Play-Based Learning

Imaginative, active and sensory play that develops creativity, social skills, and motor development.

  • Imaginative: Role-play for creativity and language.
  • Active: Running, play gym, dancing for gross motor.
  • Sensory: Water, sand, textures for cognition.
  • Outcome: Problem-solving, EQ, healthy motor growth.

Skill-Based Learning

Core competencies across literacy, numeracy, 21st‑century, social‑emotional and physical skills.

  • Cognitive: Early literacy and numeracy.
  • 21st Century: Creativity, critical thinking, collaboration.
  • Social-Emotional: Self-awareness, empathy, regulation.
  • Physical: Yoga, dance, fine motor.
  • Outcome: EI, flexibility, collaborative independence.

Value Education

Character, inclusivity, environmental stewardship and peaceful conflict resolution.

  • Character: Honesty, perseverance, teamwork.
  • Inclusivity: Respect diversity, global views.
  • Environment: Sustainability and stewardship.
  • Conflict: Peaceful resolution and perspective-taking.
  • Outcome: Responsible, compassionate citizens.

Physical Activity

Gross and fine motor development, yoga, movement, dance and rhythm for focus and wellbeing.

  • Gross Motor: Run, jump, climb, sports.
  • Fine Motor: Draw, build, tools for dexterity.
  • Yoga & Movement: Awareness and self-regulation.
  • Dance & Rhythm: Fitness with creativity.
  • Outcome: Strength, coordination, focus.

STEM-Based Learning

Hands-on science, age-appropriate technology, engineering challenges and everyday mathematics.

  • Science: Nature, weather, materials experiments.
  • Technology: Tablets, coding toys, interactive games.
  • Engineering: Structures, simple machines, inventions.
  • Mathematics: Counting, measuring, sorting, patterns.
  • Outcome: Curiosity, analytical and problem‑solving skills.

Our Programs

Fostering Growth and Curiosity at Every Stage

🌱 Playgroup Program (1.5 to 3 years)

This stage marks toddlers’ first steps toward independence. They explore the world through senses, begin feeding themselves, show curiosity, and attempt to communicate. It’s a period of rapid physical and emotional discovery, laying the foundation for preschool readiness.

    Mindful Learning Pathway

  • Motor Development: Building fine motor skills (gripping, stacking, pouring) and gross motor skills (balancing, climbing, jumping).
  • Language & Communication: Encouraging self-expression through listening, speaking, phonics, songs, and stories.
  • Exploration & Curiosity: Sensory-rich experiences that help toddlers make sense of their world.
  • Confidence Building: Activities that foster independence and reducing tantrums by improving communication.
  • Music and Movement: Yoga, gymnastics, sports, dance etc. to enhance physical and mental growth of the child.

    • Skills Being Developed

  • Eating and dressing independently
  • Fine and gross motor coordination
  • Early vocabulary and expression
  • Confidence, curiosity, and social readiness

🌟 Nursery (Pre-K) Program (2.5 to 4 years)

At this stage, children’s natural curiosity and imagination peak. They begin building friendships, developing empathy, and exploring patterns in language and math—laying the groundwork for lifelong learning.

  • Physical Development through Play: Dancing, climbing, and outdoor games to build coordination and confidence.
  • Language & Emotional Growth: Storytelling, dramatics, and show-and-tell to spark communication and empathy.
  • Cognitive Development: Early exposure to pre-math concepts (patterns, sequencing, comparing).
  • Literacy & Early Reading: Introducing alphabets, vocabulary, and self-led reading.
  • Social Emotional-Skills: Practicing collaboration, empathy, and self-confidence.

    • Skills Being Developed

  • Life skills: eating with a spoon, wearing shoes, pouring water
  • Pre-math & logic: recognizing patterns, sequencing, size comparison
  • Language & early literacy: alphabets, phonics, vocabulary
  • Social-emotional growth: empathy, collaboration, confidence
  • Motor development: building, moulding clay, balancing

🌼 K1 Program (Junior KG) (4 to 5 years)

The ages of 4–5 represent a critical window for social, emotional, physical, and cognitive development. Children are more independent, curious, and eager to connect with peers, preparing for structured learning environments.

  • Social & Emotional Learning: Circle time, shared meals, and smooth transition plans for emotional safety.
  • Physical Development: Indoor/outdoor stations, yoga, and Montessori fine-motor tools for coordination and early writing readiness.
  • Cognitive Development: Real-life explorations (nature walks, baking, classifying) and Montessori provocations for numeracy and problem-solving.
  • Language & Communication: Songs, rhymes, role-play, and phonics-base early reading and emergent writing.
  • Creative Expression: Painting, storytelling, music, and dramatic play to foster imagination.
  • Unique Features: Theme-based inquiry, bilingual exposure (English & Hindi), values-driven curriculum, and low teacher-student ratio.

    • Skills Being Developed

  • Emergent writing & pre-reading
  • Early numeracy (counting, sequencing, classifying)
  • Social confidence, empathy, and resilience
  • Fine and gross motor development
  • Creative self-expression and problem-solving
  • Independence and responsibility

🌟 K2 Program (Senior KG) (5 to 6 years)

This stage bridges preschool and formal schooling. Children grow in independence, curiosity, and social awareness, preparing for Grade 1 with nurturing guidance.

  • Academic Readiness: Introduction to writing, early math, and guided reading.
  • Social-Emotional Growth: Circle time, discussions, and mindfulness to nurture empathy and responsibility.
  • Independence & Life Skills: Managing routines, caring for belongings, and making choices confidently.
  • Creative & Critical Thinking: Group projects, role-play, and artistic expression integrated across themes.
  • Holistic Balance: Emphasis on play, movement, and imagination alongside academics.

    • Skills Being Developed

  • Emergent Literacy & Writing: Storytelling, tracing, rainbow writing, sound-sorting, independent story writing
  • Early Numeracy & Logic: Counting, sequencing, bar graphs, simple math operations
  • Language & Communication: Vocabulary building, role-play, early public speaking
  • Physical Development: Gross motor confidence, fine motor control
  • Social-Emotional Growth: Empathy, conflict resolution, collaboration
  • Creative Thinking: Dramatic play, artistic expression, problem-solving
  • Values & Self-Awareness: Kindness, responsibility, reflection, self-confidence
